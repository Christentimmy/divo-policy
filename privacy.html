Divo - Help & App Information

Version: Divo v1.0.0

About Divo

Divo is a secure, open-source voice calling application designed to provide reliable voice communication over the internet using SIP (Session Initiation Protocol). Based on the Linphone framework, Divo focuses exclusively on voice calls, offering a streamlined and intuitive calling experience while maintaining enterprise-grade security and privacy features.

What is Divo?

Divo is a voice-only communication app that allows you to make high-quality voice calls over the internet. Unlike traditional phone calls, Divo uses Voice over IP (VoIP) technology to transmit your voice data over internet connections, making it possible to call anywhere in the world at no additional cost beyond your internet connection.

The app is built with privacy and security as core principles, ensuring your conversations remain private and encrypted.

Getting Started

Initial Setup & Permissions

When you first launch Divo, you'll be guided through a simple setup process. The app requires microphone permission to enable voice calls – this is essential for the app's core functionality.

You may also be asked for contacts permission, which is optional and only used to help you easily find and call your contacts. If you deny optional permissions, the app will still work but some convenience features may be limited.

Setting Up Your SIP Account

To make calls with Divo, you'll need a SIP account. During the initial setup, you'll be prompted to complete the registration page. Once configured, your account is automatically registered with the Divo server (or your chosen SIP provider), allowing you to make and receive calls.

Making Your First Call

To make a call, open the Dialpad tab in the bottom navigation. Enter the phone number (including country code if needed), then tap the call button. Divo will automatically handle the connection process and attempt to establish a voice connection.

Main Features

Dialpad

The Dialpad provides a familiar phone keypad interface for placing calls. You can enter numbers manually and tap the call button to start a call.

Contacts Management

The Contacts tab allows you to manage and quickly access your contacts. When you grant contacts permission, Divo can read contacts from your device's address book to make calling easier. Contacts data is only used locally on your device for call initiation.

Call History

The History tab provides a log of your call activity. You can view outgoing calls along with contact name (if available), phone number, date and time of the call, and call duration. You can tap on a history entry to quickly initiate a call back to that number.

Audio Quality and Codecs

Divo supports multiple audio codecs to ensure optimal call quality. Codecs are algorithms that compress and decompress audio data, balancing sound quality and bandwidth usage. Popular codecs supported include Opus, G.722, G.711 and others.

Higher-quality codecs like Opus provide superior sound quality but may use more bandwidth, while G.711 is more compatible but uses more bandwidth. You can adjust codec preferences in the Settings tab under Audio Codecs to prioritize quality or compatibility based on your network conditions.

Settings and Customization

Audio Codec Settings

In the Settings tab, you can enable or disable specific codecs and set their priority. Keeping all codecs enabled is generally recommended for maximum compatibility. If you experience call quality issues, you may choose to disable some codecs or adjust their order.

Network and Connection

Divo automatically handles network connectivity, switching between Wi‑Fi and mobile data where available. Call quality may vary depending on network strength and latency.

Data Privacy

Divo is designed with privacy in mind. The app does not collect, store, or transmit your personal data beyond what is necessary to establish and manage calls.

  • Your contacts remain on your device and are only accessed (when permitted) to let you place calls more easily.
  • Call history is stored locally on your phone and is not uploaded to remote servers by the app.
  • Divo does not use your personal information for advertising or tracking purposes.

Open Source and Transparency

Divo is an open‑source application. Its source code is publicly available for review and contributions. Divo is based on “Linphone Belledonne Communications 2010–2025” and follows the GNU General Public License v3.0.

This transparency allows security researchers and privacy advocates to examine the code, ensuring that the app behaves as described and does not contain hidden backdoors or unauthorized data collection mechanisms.

Troubleshooting

No Audio During Calls

If you can't hear the other party or they can't hear you:

  • Check that your device volume is turned up and not muted.
  • Ensure Divo has microphone permission enabled in system settings.
  • Try switching between speakerphone and earpiece modes.
  • Verify your network connection quality (Wi‑Fi or mobile data).

Poor Call Quality

Call quality issues (choppy audio, delays, echoes) are usually related to network conditions. You can:

  • Switch between Wi‑Fi and mobile data and test again.
  • Close other apps that are using a lot of bandwidth.
  • Ensure you have a strong signal if on mobile data.
  • Adjust codec settings to prioritize compatibility in poor network conditions.

Account Registration Issues

If your SIP account fails to register:

  • Double‑check your username and password.
  • Ensure you have an active internet connection.
  • Confirm server address and any special parameters required by your SIP provider.
  • If you changed your SIP password, update it in Divo account settings.

Best Practices

  • Use a stable Wi‑Fi connection where possible for best call quality.
  • Ensure your device has adequate battery life for longer calls.
  • Keep the app updated to the latest version.
  • Review and manage your contacts regularly for a clean address book.
  • Adjust codec and network‑related settings according to your typical network conditions.
